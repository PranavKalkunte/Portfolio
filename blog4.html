Topic Selection Focused

The ikagai - a Japanese word that translates to 'a reason for being'. An intersection of four circles that can fulfill this paradigm. 'What you love' - I love working with people and providing a meaningful connection that engages a relationship. 'What you are good at' - I find myself skilled at quantitative analysis including its application in computational programs. 'What you can be paid for' - I have determined a strong career outlook for the financial world, a sizable industry with scope for profitable growth. 'What the world needs' - the world needs an affirmative stance on sustainable practices to guarantee a green world for future generations. At the center, we find a topic proposal that would be fulfilling and yet the synergy of these factors. Creating a product that increases investor access to ESG investing through asset allocation contains all four parameters. Through this project, I achieve my 'raison d'etre' - reason to be by passionately working in a vocational niche with achieves professional standards in alignment with my mission. A far fetched claim in the context of a singular program on campus that I've only been with for two months, but referencing back to my own introductory speech, creating value to make the most of life is the theme of the human spirit.
