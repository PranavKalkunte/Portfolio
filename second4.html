Research Assessment

Citation:

Sokolov, Alik, et al. “Building Machine Learning Systems for Automated ESG Scoring.” The Journal of Impact and ESG Investing, vol. 1, no. 3, 2021, pp. 39–50., https://doi.org/10.3905/jesg.2021.1.010.

Subject:

First, let us understand the main three takeaways from the publication. The authors demonstrate that there does in fact exist a feasibility and advantage to applying Natural Language Processing (NLP) for ESG. This assessment will thus expand on this feasibility in application to the final product. Using the other two takeaways, the authors assert that NLP algorithms do improve the ability of the investors. This is an affirmative proposition which provides true value in implementing the system. Finally, the authors extend themselves to discuss the algorithm design for such an ESG rating system. Since ESG is such a growing focus for asset managers, the greatest obstacle is quantitatively ascertaining these ratings. Currently, including myself, investors rely on disclosure from the companies themselves. Thus, we now also use social media and news sources beyond the conventional data collected. Once again, the advantage of quantitative finance as a growing prospect is due to its ability to analyze large swaths of data which the labor of a human could not monitor.

The goal of the approach outlined that can potentially be applied to the final product is outlined as follows. The aim is to create an NLP approach that determines the ESG content and then creates an index from news and social media. Based on the research done by the publication the order of steps that as application used by myself would be as follows.  We will use index construction based pre-filtered ESG keywords over a timeframe of a social media, Twitter for example. After sampling a training dataset and attaching labels of ESG topics. Using a BERT classifier (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) we can fine tune our model. One key helpful hint that was noted by the paper was pre-trained classifiers that can used needing only a few slight modifications such as adding embedded layers and fine-tuning the encoding.
